Four Other Schur Shows That You Should Definitely Check Out

Michael Schur may have created iconic and timeless masterpieces with The Office, Parks and Recreation, and Brookyln Nine-Nine, but he has some other notable shows that definitely deserve your attention.

Which Sitcom Boss Are You?

For every successful team, there's a successful leader, and these shows definitely have some of the best and most entertaining ones! From Michael Scott's laid-back management to Ron Swanson's stoic nature, there's a nice variety of administrative practices.

Which Parks and Rec/Brookyln Nine-Nine Character Are You?

Whether it's the dysfunctional town of Pawnee, Indiana or the chaotic streets of Brooklyn, Schur has introduced some unforgettable characters. From Amy Santiago to Andy Dwyer, there's a whole range of traits and personalities, and the best of them can be found in all of us.

Are You More like Michael or Dwight?

Two of the most iconic and memorable characters on TV, Michael Scott and Dwight Schrute are certainly personalities, and this is your chance to see where you land on that spectrum.

Lit-erally the Best Children's Books

From Little, Brown Publishing comes two children's books that imagine The Office and Parks and Rec in cartoon form! With charming illustrations and pint-sized characters, they offer heartwarming lessons and laughter for the younger generations.

Escouade 99: Brookyln Non-Non

Or in which there is a French-Canadian remake of Brookyln Nine-Nine, and the internet and cast have... thoughts. Like looking into a freakish alternate dimension, the remake is shot-to-shot similar but somehow still misses the mark.

Leslie Knope: Inspirational AF

The epitome of girl power and feminism, Leslie Knope is certainly one of the best female characters depicted on TV. Beyond simply being a good role model for young girls, Leslie's just generally a wondrous example of what it means to be a good human being. Here's a list of 5 times she tried to pass that goodness onto us.

Galentine's Day

With its humble roots as a small fictional holiday in Parks and Rec celebrating Leslie's ladyfriends, February 13th has evolved into a real holiday commemorating platonic love and friendships and honoring female power.

How Brookyln Nine-Nine Does Love Right

Brookyln Nine-Nine illustrates that romance doesn't always have to be difficult and turbulent to be interesting. Jake Peralta and Amy Santiago have one of the healthiest relationships on TV and prove that love, without all the drama and toxicity, can be truly beautiful.

John Krasinski: SNL

John Krasinski hosted the first show of Saturday Night Live of the year, and, of course, there were tons of The Office jokes. From kissing Pete Davidson (aka Pam) to lyricizing The Office theme song, this episode proved that while Krasinski may have moved onto other roles, he will always be good old Jim in our eyes.

Jacob Peralta: Musical Genius

Jake Peralta is a man of many talents, and his countless weird but fabulous songs are a clear indication of that. From the iconic Backstreet Boys cold open to his several duets with Doug Judy, here are some random scenes in which Andy Samberg really lets his vocals shine.

What Happened to Mark Brendanawicz?

Parks and Rec has introduced us to a variety of unforgettable characters, but one of the seemingly main characters has been utterly and completely forgettable. Yup, Mark Brendanawicz. Fans and the show alike have seemingly forgotten his existence with his character disappearing after the second season. What went wrong?

Stress Relief

No episode is quite as chaotic and legendary as this one. From its cold open to its satisfying ending and every little detail in between, Stress Relief quickly established itself as one of, if the the, best Office episode ever.

The Office of Brookyln

The Office has some of the most notable and memorable cast, and a few of them made a brief cameo in Brookyln Nine-Nine! While not as many as in Parks and Recreation, it's still interesting to see some familiar faces!
