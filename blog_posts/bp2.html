Jim's Best Pranks on Dwight

The Office would not be, well, The Office without the constant chaotic but very amusing workplace hijinks that occur. In that beautiful mess of brilliant mayhem is constant pranks that Jim pulls on the very gullible Dwight. Of course, there are some amazing pranks over the course of the series, but here's five of some of the best ones!

Jello Stapler

An oldie, but definitely a goodie. It's not the most elaborate prank Jim has ever pulled, but it was the first one and an amazing introduction to the dynamic between the two. The jello stapler prank became a staple of the The Office and you can even find a Funko-Pop of Dwight holding the infamous treat.

Dwight's Speech

There are many pranks that are much more elaborate than this one in the second season, but the result of Jim's "tips" on public speaking is phenomenal. While Dwight's speech did end up a lot like Mussolini's, it was definitely inspiring, and he would make an amazing dictator, for the salesmen of Northeastern Pennsylvania at least.

Impersonating Dwight

This may very well be the most iconic prank ever. The outfit is spot-on, the impersonation is perfection, and the scene gave us two amazing quotes: "Bears, beets, Battlestar Galactica‚Äù and "Identity theft is not a joke, Jim! Millions of families suffer every year!"

Bill Buttlicker

Everything about this is just pure perfection. Bill Buttlicker. Dwight being "aggressive, hostile, and definitely difficult." William M. Buttlicker. Michael falling for an imaginary million-dollar sale. Dwight being offended that he's fired because of that million-dollar sale. William M. Buttlicker, whose family built this country. It's all just perfection.

Asian Jim

Asian Jim may be one of the best cold open to ever grace the show, and Randall Park plays Jim's mannerism perfectly. The clothes, facial expression, the look into the camera, and the icing on top is the family photograph. Jim Halpert did not need to go all out, but he did, and we appreciate him for it.
